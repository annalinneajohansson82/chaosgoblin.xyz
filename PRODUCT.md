# Product

## Brand

### Identity

- **Name:** chaosgoblin.xyz
- **Tagline (working):** *A neurodivergent developer thinks out loud about AI, code, and information.*

### Personality

Technical-direct-minimal as the foundation, with a witty, honest, and personal layer. The voice is engineering-confident but human. No corporate polish, no cuteness. Authoritative but conversational.

## Users

### Primary — Recruiters & Hiring Managers

Tech employers looking for candidates in data governance, information architecture, data administration, and adjacent roles. They need to understand not just *what* I've done, but *how* I think — how I structure information, reason about complex systems, and communicate technical ideas.

The site functions as a living cover letter: every post is an implicit demonstration of those skills.

### Secondary — Neurodivergent Developers

Developers (especially AuDHD) who recognise themselves in the writing and are looking for technical depth mixed with honest reflection on how a neurodivergent brain works in engineering contexts.

## Product Purpose

A blog that publishes genuinely interesting things — currently focused on AI through the lens of a developer *and* a neurodivergent person. Underneath, it works as a portfolio and a personal, honest business card: every piece of content is also an implicit demonstration of how the author thinks, writes, and structures information.

## Positioning

This is not a tutorial mill, a personal diary, or a bare CV page. It is a hybrid:

- The content is driven by genuine curiosity — topics the author finds worth exploring
- The subtext is always professional: a recruiter reading any post should walk away with a clearer picture of who this developer is, how they reason, and whether they'd fit on a team

The site models what it advocates: calm, accessible, purposeful design that respects cognitive load while remaining typeface-forward and visually intentional.

## Anti-references

- Too busy; visual overstimulation
- Cluttered layouts with competing focal points
- Decorative elements that don't serve function
- Anything that feels corporate, generic, or optimized for algorithm-pleasing

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity through constraint** — Monochrome and typographic focus force hierarchy to earn its place.
2. **Calm over stimulation** — ADHD-friendly visual restraint; every element has breathing room.
3. **Readability as feature** — Typography and whitespace carry the design, not ornament.
4. **Utilitarian honesty** — Nothing decorative; every visual choice serves function or communication.
5. **Accessibility as baseline** — WCAG 2.2 AA is the floor, embedded from the start.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 Level AA. Core needs:
- Readable contrast and typography for cognitive accessibility
- Reduced motion support for ADHD/sensory sensitivity
- Keyboard navigation and screen reader support
- Calm, non-overwhelming visual hierarchy (ADHD consideration)
