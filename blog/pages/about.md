I've been a web developer for over 14 years. It's a career I'm proud of. But the parts I was always drawn to most weren't really about writing code.

It was always the planning phase. Structuring data. Thinking through how systems connect and where they break. Making sure the end result is genuinely usable and accessible to the people who'd actually touch it. Custom taxonomies and post types in WordPress, metadata schemas for personal data, GDPR compliance, accessibility audits. The common thread in all of it: how do you make information comprehensible, manageable, and safe?

At 42, I finally understood that I'm autistic and have ADHD. Douglas Adams had a point about that number.

It explained a lot. My brain categorizes. Finds patterns in noise. Builds systems out of apparent chaos. Taking a messy information landscape and making it structured and navigable isn't just something I can do; it's something I have genuine difficulty *not* doing. That's not a quirk. That's a skill set.

That realization pointed me in a clearer direction. I'm looking for roles in data governance, information architecture, and administration, preferably somewhere that treats accessibility and social utility as actual priorities rather than things that happen at the end when there's time.

This blog is where I think out loud about neurodivergent brains and building things on the web. Expect technical writing, honest reflection, and strong opinions about how information should be structured.
