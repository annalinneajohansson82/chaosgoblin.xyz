One unexpected advantage of ADHD hyperfocus in programming: when you lock in on a bug, you can often find it faster than methodical debugging would allow. The trade-off is you might miss lunch and lose track of time entirely.

<!--more-->

The key is recognizing when hyperfocus is helping versus when it's leading you down an unproductive rabbit hole. I've learned to use timers and deliberately interrupt myself to step back and reassess. Sometimes the best fix comes from walking away, not from grinding deeper.

The irony isn't lost on me that the same neurotype that makes me forget meetings also makes me capable of sustained focus on deeply complex problems. It's not about balance—it's about direction.
