For neurotypical people, context switching is expensive. For people with ADHD, it's catastrophic. But not in the way you might think. It's not just lost time—it's lost momentum.

<!--more-->

I can technically switch between tasks. But each switch costs me 20+ minutes to rebuild the mental model I had before. And if I'm interrupted mid-flow, that cost multiplies. The solution isn't willpower. It's ruthless protection of focus blocks.

Working async, minimizing meetings, and batching interruptions isn't a preference—it's an accommodation I've learned to normalize in ways that benefit the whole team.
